One-Zone Accretion Disk Models: Theory#

A central challenge in accretion disk modelling is that the viscous timescale,

(1)#\[t_{\rm visc} = \alpha^{-1} \left(\frac{R}{H}\right)^2 \Omega^{-1},\]

scales as \(t_{\rm visc} \propto R^{3/2}\), so different parts of the disk evolve on wildly different timescales. Rather than resolving this hierarchy with a full radial grid and adaptive timestepping, we follow Metzger et al.[1] and represent the entire disk with a single spatial zone located at the characteristic radius where the viscous timescale is comparable to the elapsed time. This one-zone approach reduces the disk evolution to a coupled two-component ODE system that can be integrated very efficiently while still capturing global viscous spreading, thermodynamic structure, and accretion onto the central object.

This document describes the theoretical framework underlying the one-zone disk models implemented in Triceratops. For usage instructions see One-Zone Accretion Disk Models.

The Core Model#

Note

We follow the development of the one-zone formalism in Metzger et al.[1].

The one-zone approximation partitions the disk into three characteristic regions based on the ratio \(t_{\rm visc}/t\):

  • Inner disk (\(t_{\rm visc} \ll t\)) — in quasi-steady state; mass is rapidly accreted and this region is dynamically subdominant.

  • Outer disk (\(t_{\rm visc} \gg t\)) — has not yet had time to evolve away from the initial conditions.

  • Middle disk (\(t_{\rm visc} \sim t\)) — currently spreading viscously and dominant in mass.

The one-zone model tracks the middle disk, treating the disk mass \(M_D\) and angular momentum \(J_D\) as its only two degrees of freedom.

One-Zone Geometry#

Following the Green’s function analysis of a viscously spreading ring [1], the area-averaged surface density and characteristic disk radius are related to the state variables by

\[M_D = A\, \pi\, \Sigma_D\, R_D^2, \qquad J_D = \frac{B}{A}\, M_D\, \sqrt{G M_{\rm BH} R_D},\]

where \(A = 1.62\) and \(B = 1.33\) are dimensionless correction constants calibrated to match the exact spreading-ring solution. Defining the ratio

\[\xi \equiv \frac{B}{A} \approx 0.821,\]

the outer disk radius and surface density follow directly from the state vector:

(2)#\[R_D = \frac{J_D^2}{\xi^2\, M_D^2\, G M_{\rm BH}}, \qquad \Sigma_D = \frac{M_D}{\pi\, A\, R_D^2}.\]

These algebraic relations are re-evaluated at every integration timestep and require no additional free parameters beyond \(M_{\rm BH}\).

Evolution Equations#

The disk mass and angular momentum evolve as

(3)#\[\begin{split}\begin{aligned} \dot{M}_D &= -\dot{M}_{\rm acc} + \dot{M}_{\rm inflow} - \dot{M}_{\rm outflow}, \\ \dot{J}_D &= -\dot{J}_{\rm acc} + \dot{J}_{\rm inflow} - \dot{J}_{\rm outflow}. \end{aligned}\end{split}\]

In the base implementation, only the accretion sink terms are active:

\[\dot{M}_{\rm acc} = f_D\,\frac{M_D}{t_{\rm visc}} = f_D\,\nu\,\frac{M_D}{R_D^2},\]

where \(\nu\) is the kinematic viscosity and

(4)#\[f_D = \frac{F_0}{1 - \sqrt{R_{\rm in}/R_D}}, \qquad F_0 = 1.6,\]

enforces a zero-torque inner boundary condition (\(F_0 = 1.6\) is the Metzger+08 calibration value). Matter draining through the inner edge at \(R_{\rm in}\) carries the local Keplerian specific angular momentum \(\ell_{\rm in} = \sqrt{G M_{\rm BH} R_{\rm in}}\), so

\[\dot{J}_{\rm acc} = \dot{M}_{\rm acc}\,\sqrt{G M_{\rm BH} R_{\rm in}}.\]

Because \(\ell_{\rm in} \ll J_D / M_D\) when \(R_{\rm in} \ll R_D\), angular momentum drains more slowly than mass and the disk outer radius grows over time — the standard viscous spreading behaviour.

Thermodynamics#

Viscous Heating and Radiative Cooling#

The thermal structure of the disk is set by balancing viscous heating against radiative cooling:

(5)#\[Q^+(T_c) = Q^-(T_c).\]

The viscous heating rate per unit area is

(6)#\[Q^+ = \frac{9}{8}\,\nu\,\Sigma\,\Omega^2 = \frac{9}{8}\,\alpha\,\frac{c_s^2}{\Omega}\,\Sigma\,\Omega^2 = \frac{9}{8}\,\alpha\,c_s^2\,\Sigma\,\Omega,\]

and the radiative cooling rate per unit area (for an optically thick disk in the diffusion limit) is

(7)#\[Q^- = \sigma_{\rm SB}\,T_{\rm eff}^4.\]

The midplane temperature \(T_c\) and effective temperature \(T_{\rm eff}\) are related through radiative diffusion:

(8)#\[T_c^4 = \frac{3}{4}\,\tau\,T_{\rm eff}^4,\]

where the optical depth \(\tau = \kappa\,\Sigma\) depends on the opacity prescription.

Energy Balance Residual#

Combining (6), (7), and (8) and substituting \(\nu = \alpha c_s^2/\Omega\), the energy balance (5) reduces to a single equation in \(T_c\):

(9)#\[\mathcal{F}(T_c) \equiv \ln Q_0 + 2\ln c_s(T_c) - 4\ln T_c = 0,\]

where the quantity

(10)#\[Q_0 \equiv \frac{27}{32}\,\frac{\alpha\,\kappa\,\Sigma^2\,\Omega}{\sigma_{\rm SB}}\]

collects all terms that do not depend on \(T_c\). The sound speed \(c_s(T_c)\) is determined by the equation of state and depends on the thermodynamic closure (see below).

Note

All quantities in (9) are evaluated in log-space internally to avoid floating-point underflow for the extreme temperatures (\(10\,{\rm K} \lesssim T_c \lesssim 10^{14}\,{\rm K}\)) that arise across the full range of one-zone disk models.

Solution Strategy#

The form of (9) depends on whether \(c_s(T_c)\) can be expressed analytically.

For a pure ideal-gas equation of state,

\[c_s^2 = \frac{k_B T_c}{\mu m_p},\]

the residual (9) becomes

\[\ln Q_0 + \ln\frac{k_B T_c}{\mu m_p} - 4\ln T_c = 0.\]

Rearranging:

\[\ln Q_0 + \ln\frac{k_B}{\mu m_p} = 3\ln T_c,\]

giving a closed-form solution:

(11)#\[\boxed{ T_c = \left(\frac{Q_0\, k_B}{\mu\, m_p}\right)^{1/3}. }\]

No root-finding is needed; the Cython closure evaluates this formula directly at every timestep.

When radiation pressure is included, the equation of state is

\[P = \frac{\rho k_B T_c}{\mu m_p} + \frac{a T_c^4}{3},\]

and \(c_s^2 = P/\rho\). The midplane density is found from vertical hydrostatic equilibrium,

\[\rho = \frac{\Sigma}{2 H} = \frac{\Sigma\,\Omega}{2\,c_s},\]

which gives a quadratic relation between \(c_s\) and \(T_c\) for fixed \(\Sigma\) and \(\Omega\):

(12)#\[c_s^2 - \frac{2\,a\,T_c^4}{3\,\Sigma\,\Omega}\,c_s - \frac{k_B T_c}{\mu m_p} = 0.\]

The physically meaningful (positive) root is

\[c_s(T_c) = \frac{D + \sqrt{D^2 + 4k_B T_c/(\mu m_p)}}{2}, \qquad D \equiv \frac{2\,a\,T_c^4}{3\,\Sigma\,\Omega}.\]

Substituting this into (9) yields a transcendental equation in \(T_c\) that must be solved numerically. Triceratops uses a two-step procedure:

  1. Bracket expansion — starting from an initial guess \(T_c^{\rm guess}\) derived from the gas-only formula, the bracket \([T_{\rm lo}, T_{\rm hi}]\) is expanded geometrically until \(\mathcal{F}\) changes sign.

  2. Brent’s method — once a bracket is established, Brent’s method converges to the root with a mix of bisection and inverse quadratic interpolation, guaranteeing superlinear convergence.

Both steps are implemented in Cython and run entirely without the GIL.

Important

For physical disk parameters there is always exactly one root: \(\mathcal{F}\) is a monotone function of \(T_c\) because \(c_s\) grows more slowly than \(T_c\) in both the gas-dominated (\(c_s \propto T_c^{1/2}\)) and radiation-dominated (\(c_s \propto T_c\)) limits. If no root is found, the integrator returns an EXPAND_FAIL or NO_BRACKET error (see One-Zone Accretion Disk Models for the full error code reference).

Viscosity#

Given \(T_c\) and therefore \(c_s\), the kinematic viscosity follows from the Shakura–Sunyaev \(\alpha\)-prescription:

\[\nu = \alpha\,c_s\,H = \frac{\alpha\,c_s^2}{\Omega},\]

and the disk scale height is

\[H = \frac{c_s}{\Omega}.\]

The viscous timescale that sets the integration timestep is

\[t_{\rm visc} = \frac{R_D^2}{\nu}.\]

Numerical Architecture#

The hot integration loop in Triceratops is a compiled Cython explicit-Euler kernel (run_one_zone_model()) that advances the state vector \((M_D, J_D)\) by one step at a time. For each step the kernel executes the following operations in strict order:

  1. Geometry — compute \(R_D\), \(\Sigma\), \(\Omega\) from the current state using (2).

  2. Closure — call the thermodynamic closure (a C function pointer injected at construction time) to solve (9) for \(T_c\) and to derive all thermodynamic and viscous quantities: \(T_{\rm eff}\), \(\tau\), \(c_s\), \(\nu\), \(t_{\rm visc}\).

  3. Derivative — evaluate \((\dot{M}_D, \dot{J}_D)\) using the viscosity from step 2.

  4. Write — record all quantities to the result array at the current column.

  5. Euler update — \(M_D \leftarrow M_D + \dot{M}_D \Delta t\), \(J_D \leftarrow J_D + \dot{J}_D \Delta t\), with the adaptive timestep \(\Delta t = f_D \cdot t_{\rm visc}\).

The entire loop runs without the GIL, making it safe to call from multi-threaded contexts.

Closure extensibility is achieved through the OneZoneClosure extension type. Each concrete model subclass implements _build_cython_closure() to return a closure object with three C function pointers installed: a closure function (solves the energy balance), a derivative function (evaluates the ODE right-hand side), and a writer function (serialises the full state to the result array). The base class and integrator kernel are completely independent of the specific physics choice.

Implemented Closures#

Gas Pressure, Electron-Scattering Opacity#

The simplest physically self-consistent closure within the one-zone framework follows Metzger et al.[1] and assumes:

  • Opacity: pure electron scattering, \(\kappa = \kappa_{\rm es} = 0.34\,{\rm cm^2\,g^{-1}}\).

  • Pressure: ideal gas, \(P = \rho k_B T_c / (\mu m_p)\).

The energy balance (9) then has the closed-form solution (11). The full solution sequence per timestep is:

  1. Evaluate \(Q_0 = (27/32)\,\alpha\,\kappa_{\rm es}\,\Sigma^2\,\Omega / \sigma_{\rm SB}\).

  2. Solve analytically: \(T_c = (Q_0 k_B / \mu m_p)^{1/3}\).

  3. Compute the effective temperature from (8): \(T_{\rm eff} = (T_c^4 / [(3/4)\kappa_{\rm es}\Sigma])^{1/4}\).

  4. Compute the sound speed: \(c_s = \sqrt{k_B T_c / (\mu m_p)}\).

  5. Compute the viscosity: \(\nu = \alpha c_s^2 / \Omega\).

This closure is implemented in GasPressureElectronScatteringDisk.

Gas + Radiation Pressure, Electron-Scattering Opacity#

This closure extends the gas-only case by including radiation pressure in the equation of state:

\[P = P_{\rm gas} + P_{\rm rad} = \frac{\rho k_B T_c}{\mu m_p} + \frac{a T_c^4}{3}.\]

The sound speed \(c_s(T_c)\) is found from the quadratic (12), and the energy balance (9) is solved for \(T_c\) using bracket expansion followed by Brent’s method. The gas-only analytic formula provides the initial bracket centre.

In the gas-pressure-dominated limit \((a T_c^4 / 3 \ll \rho k_B T_c / \mu m_p)\), the two closures agree. Differences become significant when

\[\frac{P_{\rm rad}}{P_{\rm gas}} \sim \frac{a T_c^3 \mu m_p}{3 \rho k_B} \gtrsim 1,\]

which occurs at high temperatures or low densities (high \(\alpha\), high accretion rate, or large disk radius).

This closure is implemented in FullPressureElectronScatteringDisk.
