Electron Cooling#

These examples cover the radiative cooling of relativistic electrons in Trilobite: synchrotron cooling rates, the time-evolving cooling Lorentz factor \(\gamma_c\), the transition between fast- and slow-cooling regimes, and the role of inverse Compton losses (quantified by the Compton \(Y\) parameter) relative to synchrotron losses.

These examples are most relevant when modeling sources at late times (where cooling can reshape the electron distribution) or when IC losses are non-negligible.
