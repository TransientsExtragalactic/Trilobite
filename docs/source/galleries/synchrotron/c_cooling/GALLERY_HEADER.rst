Electron Cooling
================

These examples cover the radiative cooling of relativistic electrons in Triceratops:
synchrotron cooling rates, the time-evolving cooling Lorentz factor :math:`\gamma_c`,
the transition between fast- and slow-cooling regimes, and the role of inverse Compton
losses (quantified by the Compton :math:`Y` parameter) relative to synchrotron losses.

These examples are most relevant when modeling sources at late times (where cooling can
reshape the electron distribution) or when IC losses are non-negligible.
