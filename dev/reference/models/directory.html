Triceratops Model Directory#

Triceratops provides an ever-expanding collection of forward models designed for astrophysical inference, phenomenological curve fitting, and physically motivated transient modeling.

The goal of the model library is twofold:

  • Provide robust, reusable forward models for inference workflows

  • Offer modular building blocks that can be extended, subclassed, or composed

Models span a range of complexity, from lightweight mathematical curve-fitting forms to physically grounded shock and emission models. All models share a unified interface through the Model base class, ensuring consistent parameter handling, unit support, and compatibility with likelihood and inference tools.

If you are new to the framework, we recommend first reading the Triceratops Models documentation, which explains:

  • The design philosophy of the modeling framework

  • The role of ModelParameter and ModelVariable

  • How unit handling and coercion are implemented

  • How models integrate with inference engines

  • Best practices for implementing new models

Below is the catalog of existing models, organized by category. Each model includes a link to its documentation and source code.

Hint

Even if we do not yet provide the exact model you need, many models are designed to be easily subclassed or extended. Most phenomenological models can be adapted with minimal effort. Physically motivated models are built to expose their core components so that researchers can modify assumptions cleanly.

Generic Models#

Generic models are designed to be flexible, modular, and easily subclassed. They are ideal for rapid prototyping, exploratory data analysis, and building custom likelihood functions. These models are not tied to specific physical scenarios but provide mathematical forms that can be adapted to a wide range of applications.

Curve-Fitting Models#

These models provide flexible mathematical forms for fitting data. They are especially useful during exploratory analysis and for building likelihood functions.

Generic Broken Power Laws

BrokenPowerLaw

Sharp two-segment broken power-law model.

SmoothedBrokenPowerLaw

Smooth two-segment broken power-law model.

TripleBrokenPowerLaw

Sharp three-segment broken power-law model.

SmoothedTripleBrokenPowerLaw

Smooth three-segment broken power-law model.

Light Curve Models#

These phenomenological models are designed for time-domain transient analysis and pulse-shape modeling.

Generic Light Curve Models

FRED

Fast Rise, Exponential Decay (FRED) lightcurve model.

GeneralizedFRED

Generalized Fast Rise, Exponential Decay (FRED) lightcurve model.

GaussianPulse

Symmetric Gaussian pulse model with constant background.

LogNormalPulse

Log-normal asymmetric pulse model with constant background.

BrokenPowerLawTime

Sharp broken power-law temporal model with constant background.

SmoothedBrokenPowerLawTime

Smoothly broken power-law temporal model.

ExponentialRisePowerLawDecay

Exponential rise with power-law decay model.

NorrisPulse

Norris GRB pulse model.

WeibullPulse

Weibull pulse profile with background.

LogisticPulse

Logistic sigmoid transition model with background.

Evolving Phenomenological Models#

Models for phenomenological modeling (not tied to specific physical scenarios) that include time-evolution of parameters.

Generic SED Evolution

PL_Evolving_SBPL_Model

A generic model of synchrotron SED evolution assuming a time-evolving smoothed broken power law.

BPL_Evolving_SBPL_Model

A generic model of synchrotron SED evolution assuming a time-evolving broken power law in time.

TripleBPL_Evolving_SBPL_Model

A generic model of synchrotron SED evolution assuming a time-evolving triple broken power law in time.

SED Models#

SED models are designed to provide full-physics spectral energy distribution modeling for various different scenarios, emission mechanisms, and physical assumptions. These models are ideal for multi-wavelength fitting and inference.

Synchrotron

SSA_Cooling_SynchrotronSEDModel

Physical synchrotron spectral energy distribution including radiative cooling and synchrotron self-absorption (SSA).

SSA_SynchrotronSEDModel

Physical synchrotron spectral energy distribution including SSA but without radiative cooling.

Cooling_SynchrotronSEDModel

Physical synchrotron spectral energy distribution including cooling but without synchrotron self-absorption.

SynchrotronSEDModel

Optically thin synchrotron spectral energy distribution without radiative cooling or self-absorption.

Synchrotron_SSA_SBPL_Model

Phenomenological synchrotron self-absorbed broken power-law spectral energy distribution (SED).

Supernova Models#

These models incorporate physically motivated shock dynamics, radiation processes, and circumstellar interaction physics.

Self-Similar Shock Models

chevalier_shock.ChevalierShockModel

Supernova shock synchrotron emission model based on Chevalier (1982).
