Free-Free Absorption of Synchrotron Emission
=============================================

When a synchrotron-emitting source is surrounded by ionized gas — as in a radio supernova
embedded in a dense stellar wind — free-free absorption (FFA) creates a low-frequency
turnover that is distinct from synchrotron self-absorption (SSA).  Unlike the
:math:`F_\nu \propto \nu^{5/2}` SSA rise, the FFA-absorbed spectrum rises steeply
as :math:`F_\nu \propto \nu^2` below the absorption turnover.

These examples show how to construct an FFA-attenuated synchrotron SED using the
wind optical depth functions from :mod:`triceratops.radiation.free_free.absorption`.
