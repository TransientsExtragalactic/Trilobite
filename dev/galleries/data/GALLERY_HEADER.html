Working With Observational Data#

These examples demonstrate how to load, inspect, and visualize observational data in Trilobite using the container classes in trilobite.data. They cover the full range of supported data types — radio photometry, radio light curves, optical photometry, and optical light curves — as well as the bridge from validated containers to the inference pipeline.

What you’ll find here

  • Constructing RadioPhotometryContainer objects, grouping observations into epochs, and plotting SEDs.

  • Working with RadioLightCurveContainer for single-frequency time-series data.

  • Building multi-band OpticalPhotometryContainer objects and extracting per-band light curves.

  • Using OpticalLightCurveContainer for flux/magnitude dual representation.

  • Converting any container to InferenceData and understanding its structure before wiring into a likelihood.

  • Extracting per-band light curves from multi-frequency photometry with extract_lightcurve() and extract_lightcurve().
