****************************
Free-Free Emission Examples
****************************

These examples cover thermal bremsstrahlung (free-free) radiation in Triceratops — from the
Gaunt factor corrections that shape the spectrum, through flux prediction for a free-free
emitting plasma, to the free-free absorption that modifies synchrotron SEDs at low frequencies.

Examples are grouped into three sections:

- **Gaunt Factors** — comparing the analytic and tabulated approximations across frequency and temperature
- **Free-Free Emission** — computing the flux density and spectrum from a thermal plasma
- **Free-Free Absorption and Synchrotron** — applying a free-free absorbing screen to a synchrotron SED

.. rubric:: Theory and API

For the underlying physics and derivations, see :ref:`free_free_theory`.
For the full API reference, see :ref:`free_free_emission`.

Gaunt Factors
=============

The free-free Gaunt factor :math:`g_{\rm ff}(\nu, T)` is the quantum-mechanical correction
to the classical bremsstrahlung cross-section. These examples compare the four approximations
available in Triceratops — two analytic fits and two tabulated interpolators — and show where
they agree or diverge across the radio-to-X-ray frequency range and over many decades of
temperature.

**Theory:** :ref:`free_free_theory`

**API:** :ref:`free_free_emission`

Free-Free Emission and Flux Prediction
=======================================

These examples demonstrate how to compute the observed flux density from a thermal
free-free emitting plasma.  Starting from the optical depth profile, they build up to
the full radiative transfer solution and show how the characteristic
:math:`F_\nu \propto \nu^2` (optically thick) to :math:`F_\nu \propto \nu^{-0.1}`
(optically thin) transition depends on the emission measure and plasma temperature.

**Theory:** :ref:`free_free_theory`

**API:** :ref:`free_free_emission`

Free-Free Absorption of Synchrotron Emission
=============================================

When a synchrotron-emitting source is surrounded by ionized gas — as in a radio supernova
embedded in a dense stellar wind — free-free absorption (FFA) creates a low-frequency
turnover that is distinct from synchrotron self-absorption (SSA).  Unlike the
:math:`F_\nu \propto \nu^{5/2}` SSA rise, the FFA-absorbed spectrum rises steeply
as :math:`F_\nu \propto \nu^2` below the absorption turnover.

These examples show how to construct an FFA-attenuated synchrotron SED using the
wind optical depth functions from :mod:`triceratops.radiation.free_free.absorption`.

**Theory:** :ref:`free_free_theory`, :ref:`synch_sed_theory`

**API:** :ref:`free_free_emission`, :ref:`synchrotron_seds`
