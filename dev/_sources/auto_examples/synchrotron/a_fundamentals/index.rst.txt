Synchrotron Fundamentals
========================

These examples explore the foundational building blocks of synchrotron radiation:
the kernel functions :math:`F(x)` and :math:`G(x)` that describe single-electron emission,
the central :math:`\nu \propto \gamma^2 B` frequency relation, and how a power-law electron
population produces a power-law radio spectrum.

These examples are the right starting point if you want to understand *why* synchrotron spectra
look the way they do before working with the higher-level SED classes.

**Theory:** :ref:`synchrotron_theory`

**API:** :ref:`synchrotron_core`
