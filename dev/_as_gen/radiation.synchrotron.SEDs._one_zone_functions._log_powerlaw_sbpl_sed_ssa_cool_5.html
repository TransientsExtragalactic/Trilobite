radiation.synchrotron.SEDs._one_zone_functions._log_powerlaw_sbpl_sed_ssa_cool_5#

radiation.synchrotron.SEDs._one_zone_functions._log_powerlaw_sbpl_sed_ssa_cool_5(log_nu: ndarray | list[float] | tuple[float, ...], log_nu_m: float, log_nu_c: float, log_nu_a: float, log_nu_ac: float, log_nu_max: float, p: float, s: float)[source]#

Synchrotron SED with synchrotron self-absorption (SSA) in the fast-cooling regime.

Includes stratified absorption, assuming the ordering

\[\nu < \nu_{ac} < \nu_a < \nu_c < \nu_m.\]

In this regime, electrons cool efficiently below the injection energy, and synchrotron self-absorption occurs within a stratified, cooling electron population behind the shock. This produces an additional low-frequency break at \(\nu_{ac}\), separating optically thick emission from uncooled and cooled electron layers.

The resulting spectral segments are:

  • \(F_\nu \propto \nu^2\) for \(\nu < \nu_{ac}\),

  • \(F_\nu \propto \nu^{11/8}\) for \(\nu_{ac} < \nu < \nu_a\),

  • \(F_\nu \propto \nu^{1/3}\) for \(\nu_a < \nu < \nu_c\),

  • \(F_\nu \propto \nu^{-1/2}\) for \(\nu_c < \nu < \nu_m\),

  • \(F_\nu \propto \nu^{-p/2}\) for \(\nu > \nu_m\).

The spectrum is anchored at the cooling frequency \(\nu_c\), which is the dominant physical break in the fast-cooling regime. Lower-frequency structure is constructed via scale-free smoothed broken power laws (SFBPLs) to ensure continuity and correct asymptotic behavior.

Parameters:

  • log_nu (array-like) – Logarithm of the frequencies at which to evaluate the SED.

  • log_nu_m (float) – Logarithm of the injection frequency \(\nu_m\).

  • log_nu_c (float) – Logarithm of the cooling frequency \(\nu_c\).

  • log_nu_a (float) – Logarithm of the synchrotron self-absorption frequency \(\nu_a\).

  • log_nu_ac (float) – Logarithm of the stratified absorption break frequency \(\nu_{ac}\).

  • log_nu_max (float) – Logarithm of the maximum synchrotron frequency.

  • p (float) – Electron energy distribution index.

  • s (float) – Smoothness parameter for SFBPL transitions.

Returns:

Logarithm of the synchrotron SED.

Return type:

array-like

Notes

  • Assumes fast cooling (\(\nu_c \ll \nu_m\)) and the stated frequency ordering; no internal validation is performed.

  • The \(11/8\) slope arises from stratified synchrotron self-absorption in a cooling electron population.
